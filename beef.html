Slow Cooked Pot Roast

Dust off that crockpot. Serves 8. Or just one guy eating tender beef for days. I am using my favorite cooking blog's recipe, which can be found here.

Ingredients

  • 5 lbs beef chuck roast, preferably with bone
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 3 tablespoons flour, or as needed
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 8 oz sliced mushrooms
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 2 1/2 cups chicken broth
  • 3 medium carrots, chopped
  • 2 stalks celery, chopped
  • 1 sprig rosemary
  • 2 sprigs thyme

Directions

  1. Put the chopped carrots and celery into the slow cooker.
  2. Season roast generously with salt and pepper. Sprinkle flour on both sides and pat into meat. Shake off excess flour.
  3. In a large skillet, heat vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Sear the roast on both sides for about 6 minutes each, or until well browned. Place meat on top of celery and carrots.
  4. Back to the skillet, reduce heat to medium. Stir in mushrooms and butter; cook for 3-4 minutes.
  5. Stir in onion; cook onions until translucent and begin to brown; about 5 minutes. Add garlic; cook for a minute.
  6. Stir in half of flour; cook for about a minute. Stir in tomato paste; cook for about a minute.
  7. Add chicken stock, mix well. Cook until simmer, then remove from heat.
  8. Pour delicious gravy mixture over beef in slow cooker. Add rosemary and thyme.
  9. Cover slow cooker. Cook on high for 6 hours, or until meat is as tender as you prefer.
  10. Skim off any fat during the cooking process and remove any bones. Season with salt and pepper.
I serve over mashed potatoes, but goes great over rice or by itself.
